Dr. Steven Farrell

I am a particle physicist working on the ATLAS experiment, a general purpose particle detector experiment at the Large Hadron Collider. I work with a large international collaboration of scientists to study the fundamental particles of nature and their interactions. We collide protons at extremely high energies and measure the particles produced in the collisions. We then sift through the massive amount of collected data to validate features of our Standard Model of particle physics as well as search for evidence of new physics beyond the Standard Model. In particular, I look for evidence of supersymmetry, a proposed extension of the Standard Model.

For an overview of the work I've done on ATLAS, you may find my thesis here. Also, my thesis defense slides are available here.
